# Staff Engineering Thread

You do not become staff by only learning more technology.

You become staff by getting better at:

- choosing the right problem
- shaping scope
- writing down decisions
- aligning people
- reducing risk
- creating leverage for others

## What Changes Between Senior And Staff

Senior often means:

- stronger execution in a bounded area
- reliable technical delivery

Staff requires more:

- broader problem framing
- cross-cutting architecture judgment
- better prioritization under ambiguity
- written influence
- operational ownership

## Weekly Artifact Plan

### Week 1

- product brief
- architecture overview

### Week 2

- ADR: provider abstraction and request flow

### Week 3

- retrieval design review
- ADR: retrieval and indexing strategy

### Week 4

- state and memory note

### Week 5

- RFC: when multi-agent is warranted

### Week 6

- risk register
- quality review

### Week 7

- launch checklist
- go/no-go criteria

### Week 8

- postmortem
- portfolio case study
- promotion-style evidence summary

## Staff Habits To Build

- write before building when the decision is expensive
- document why, not just what
- cut scope aggressively
- make uncertainty visible
- tie technical work to business or user outcomes
- leave behind reusable systems, not one-off heroics

## Proof By Day 60

You should finish with:

- a product brief someone else could understand
- architecture docs that explain trade-offs
- ADRs that show judgment
- a launch-quality checklist
- a postmortem that shows ownership
- a clear portfolio narrative of problem, design, build, measure, and iterate
